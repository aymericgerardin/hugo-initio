A club's evening

A Toastmasters meeting has 4 parts:

  1. Improvisation: We start each evening with table topics. Table TopicsÂ® is a long-standing Toastmasters tradition intended to help members develop their ability to organize their thoughts quickly and respond to an impromptu question or topic.
  2. Prepared Speech: Then we move forward with the speeches prepared by our memebers. Preparing for a speech is one of the best ways to ensure you give an effective presentation.
  3. Evaluation: We progress thanks to feedbacks. Public speaking and leadership are skills that can be developed and improved. In Toastmasters, feedback is called evaluation, and it is the heart of the Toastmasters educational program.
  4. Networking: After the sesion we meet together for a diner or a drink

Where leaders are made

Develop your leadership with leaderhip roles in club meetings or in club :

  • Toastmaster of the day: Play the master of ceremony requieres experience! Taking on this role improves organization, time management and public speaking skills.
  • General evaluator: The General Evaluator evaluates everything that takes place during the club meeting. Taking on this role improves critical thinking, organization, time management, motivational and team-building skills
  • Evaluator: You observe the speeches and leadership roles of your fellow club members and offer evaluations of their efforts, and they do the same for you. Taking on this role improves active listening, critical thinking and positive feedback skills.
  • Objective Evaluations : Ah-Counter, Grammarian, Timer provide objective evaluations on key topics for public speaking
